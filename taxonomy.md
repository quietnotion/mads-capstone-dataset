# Subject Category Taxonomy

Categories were designed to keep the chart readable. Too many single project buckets make for a useless visualization. The current set of eleven was calibrated against the first 69 projects (May 2025 through April 2026). Expect it to evolve.

## Current categories

**Healthcare and Biomedical.** Clinical and biomedical applications. Disease prediction, drug safety, medical imaging, public health surveys, genomics, biotech.

**Finance and Markets.** Asset pricing, portfolio allocation, market regime detection, sentiment for trading, financial document processing, crypto, prediction markets.

**NLP and LLMs.** Text analysis, language models, sentiment, topic modeling, summarization, chatbots, fine tuning.

**Recommendation Systems.** Any project where the main output is suggesting an item. Books, movies, recipes, cities, FEMA resources.

**Environment and Energy.** Ocean health, wildfires, solar, batteries, water treatment.

**Social Sciences and Networks.** Education, labor economics, academic collaboration, dating preferences, food network analysis. The "people as data" bucket when nothing more specific fits.

**Transportation and Safety.** Traffic, crashes, vehicle safety, road infrastructure.

**Real Estate and Property.** Housing, commercial leases, property valuation.

**Sports and Entertainment.** Athletic performance, recruiting, NIL, the film industry, games.

**Software and Data Engineering.** Tools for teams that ship systems. Security, data architecture, internal tooling.

**Computer Vision and IoT.** Image or sensor based applications that aren't healthcare.

## Judgment calls

Some projects sit on a boundary. These were the close ones.

**Kalshi NFL price predictions → Finance.** Sentiment analysis on NFL content, output is a price prediction for a prediction market. Finance wins because the stakeholder is a trader.

**Financial Form CNN → Finance.** CNN is the technique, finance is the domain. When the domain is that dominant, it wins.

**Flavor Saviors ingredient networks → Social Sciences and Networks.** Network analysis methodology outweighs the food domain. If more food projects show up in future cohorts, this deserves its own category.

**Gig Work Economic Stress → Social Sciences and Networks.** Labor economics. Folded in to avoid a category for a single project. If a second economics project shows up, Economics earns its own row.

**Greenlight Predicting Film Financial Success → Sports and Entertainment.** Output is a financial prediction, but the stakeholders and domain are the entertainment industry. Entertainment wins over Finance here.

**BandPass Egg Fertility Hyperspectral Imaging → Computer Vision and IoT.** Hyperspectral imaging is the methodology, egg grading is the application. No other agriculture projects yet, so no dedicated Agriculture category.

**Queue-rious Minds Ticket AI → Software and Data Engineering.** Uses NLP under the hood, but the product is an IT operations tool. Internal tooling framing wins.

## Merging logic

- Three or more projects share a domain → that domain gets its own category.
- One or two → fold into the nearest thematically adjacent category with a note.
- None of the adjacent categories fit → leave as a stub and revisit when another cohort posts.

If a merge looks wrong, open an issue. I will rethink it.

## Categories that might split later

Watching these clusters as more cohorts post:

- **Social Sciences and Networks** is doing a lot of work. If education or labor economics hit three projects each, they split off.
- **Software and Data Engineering** could split into Security and Data Infrastructure if the sample grows.
- A dedicated **Agriculture and Food** category becomes viable if the next cohort adds two more projects there.
- **Fitness and Wearables** does not exist yet. No projects to date. If three Strava / Oura / wearable projects show up, it earns a row.
